Biography

I am a Spanish-born scientist with interents covering a broad range of topics in observational cosmology and galaxy formation physics. My family is from Extremadura, a beautiful region in the central-western part of the Iberian Peninsula. I grew up in the UNESCO World Heritage city of Cáceres, which presents dozens of medieval buildings and is considered the Third Monumental Complex of Europe. In 2007, I moved to Salmanca to study a B.S. in Physics at the University of Salamanca, which is the oldest Hispanic university and the third oldest in the world. After graduating, I moved to Madrid to study an M.Sc. in Astrophysics at the Autonomous University of Madrid.I finished this master in 2013, and a couple of months later I started a Ph.D. in Cosmology at Centro de Estudios de Física del Cosmos de Aragón (CEFCA). My supervisors were Carlos Hernández-Monteagudo and Raúl Angulo, and I also worked closely with Silvia Bonoli. After defending my thesis at Complutense University of Madrid in 2017 I moved to Chicago to start a three years postdoctoral fellowship in the Cosmological Physics and Advanced Computing (CPAC) group at the Argonne National Laboratory. During this time, I worked closely with Lindsey Bleem, Salman Habib, Andrew Hearin, Katrin Heitmann, Nesar Ramachandra, and other members of the group. Since 2020, I am a postdoc at the Donostia International Physics Center (DIPC).
