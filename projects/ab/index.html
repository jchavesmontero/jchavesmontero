Assembly bias

A long-standing prediction of structure formation theories is that the clustering of dark matter halos only depends upon their mass. However, cosmological simulations show that the clustering of halos depends upon their concentration, spin, formation time, etc. This effect is commonly known as halo assembly bias and it has been shown to also affect galaxy clustering. I am working on studying the origin of this effect and its influence on the large-scale distribution of galaxies.

Publications:

  • Montero-Dorta A. D., Chaves-Montero J., Artale M. C., Favole G., 2021, arXiv, arXiv:2105.05274.
    Summary: strong connection between the specifict mass accretion history of dark matter halos and the properties of the galaxies that these harbor. The galaxy assembly bias signal of the progenitors of galaxies selected according to this property increases with redshift up to at least z=4.
  • Contreras S., Chaves-Montero J., Zennaro M., Angulo R. E., 2021, arXiv, arXiv:2105.05854.
    Summary: the strength of halo assembly bias does not depend on cosmology, and galaxy assembly bias depends much strongly on galaxy formation physics than cosmology.
  • Chaves-Montero J., Angulo R. E., Schaye J., Schaller M., Crain R. A., Furlong M., Theuns T., 2016, MNRAS, 460, 3100. doi:10.1093/mnras/stw1225.
    Summary: for the first time, galaxy assembly bias is found in an hydrodynamical simulations. The precision of different models capturing this effect depends on the particularities of the galaxy-halo connection chosen.
